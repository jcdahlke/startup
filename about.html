    What is RGB?

    RGB stands for Red, Green, and Blue, which are the three primary colors of light used in digital displays like computer monitors, TVs, and phone screens. By mixing different intensities of these three colors, we can create a wide range of other colors.

    Hereâ€™s how it works:

    • Red, Green, and Blue are the base colors.
    • Each color can have a value between 0 (no intensity) and 255 (full intensity).
    • When you combine different values of these three colors, you get different colors. For example
    • Full red (255, 0, 0) gives you red.
    • Full green (0, 255, 0) gives you green.
    • Full blue (0, 0, 255) gives you blue.
    • Combining red, green, and blue at full intensity (255, 255, 255) gives you white.
    • No light at all (0, 0, 0) results in black.
    • This system is the foundation for most of the colors you see on digital screens!
